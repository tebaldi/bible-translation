1 Who has believed what they heard from us?<N1> And to whom has the arm of the LORD been revealed?  { (1) Or [Who has believed what we have heard?]  }
2 For he grew up before him like a young plant, and like a root out of dry ground; he had no form or majesty that we should look at him, and no beauty that we should desire him.
3 He was despised and rejected<N1> by men; a man of sorrows,<N2> and acquainted with<N3> grief;<N4> and as one from whom men hide their faces<N5> he was despised, and we esteemed him not.  { (1) Or [forsaken]  (2) Or [pains]; also verse 4   (3) Or [and knowing]  (4) Or [sickness]; also verse 4   (5) Or [as one who hides his face from us]  }
4 Surely he has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows; yet we esteemed him stricken, smitten by God, and afflicted.
5 But he was wounded for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities; upon him was the chastisement that brought us peace, and with his stripes we are healed.
6 All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way; and the LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all.
7 He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth; like a lamb that is led to the slaughter, and like a sheep that before its shearers is silent, so he opened not his mouth.
8 By oppression and judgment he was taken away; and as for his generation, who considered that he was cut off out of the land of the living, stricken for the transgression of my people?
9 And they made his grave with the wicked and with a rich man in his death, although he had done no violence, and there was no deceit in his mouth.
10 Yet it was the will of the LORD to crush him; he has put him to grief;<N1> when his soul makes<N2> an offering for sin, he shall see his offspring; he shall prolong his days; the will of the LORD shall prosper in his hand.  { (1) Or [he has made him sick]  (2) Or [when you make his soul]  }
11 Out of the anguish of his soul he shall see<N1> and be satisfied; by his knowledge shall the righteous one, my servant, make many to be accounted righteous, and he shall bear their iniquities.  { (1) Masoretic Text; Dead Sea Scroll [he shall see light]  }
12 Therefore I will divide him a portion with the many,<N1> and he shall divide the spoil with the strong,<N2> because he poured out his soul to death and was numbered with the transgressors; yet he bore the sin of many, and makes intercession for the transgressors.  { (1) Or [with the great]  (2) Or [with the numerous]  }
